Africa

Botswana

Blessed with some of the greatest wildlife spectacles on earth, this is one of the great safari destinations in Africa. Here there are free ranging big cats, African wild dogs, aquatic antelopes, majestic rhinos, abundant bird-life and a multitude of elephants.

The landscapes vary from the Okavango Delta to the Kalahari Desert and everything in between. It is one of the more politically and ideologically enlightened countries in sub-Saharan Africa.

Kenya

The Africa you always dreamed of, a land with vast savannahs, massive herds of wildlife and people with proud traditions from the beings of human society. Here there are families fo elephants wandering the swamps below Mount Kilimanjaro, lakes shallows filled with immense numbers of vibrant pink flamingos and silent predators stalking their prey.

Yet there are hidden depths to discover in Kenya. Here the indigenous peoples struggle to maintain their culture as the modern world intrudes on their lands. Though the country is low-income people approach life with great warmth and exuberance.

Malawi

The landscape here is ever changing; from the tips of lofty mountains, descending deep escarpments, through peaceful woodland, farmland and dry swaying grassland to the shores of a shimmering lake. Fish eagles can be heard calling from baobab trees from the gentle sands. The views here are unforgettable.

Malawi is referred to as Africa's next big destination. It has the Big Five safari animals and the breathtaking landscapes.

Mozambique

This mysterious African country isn't visited by many tourists, but has much to experience for those who explore here. It beckons with its coastline, swaying palms, long beaches edged by dunes, traditions, vibe, culture and adventure. Here there are shoals of colourful fish swimming in turquoise waters, vibrant corals, idyllic archipelagos, crashing surf and vast forests home to lions, elephants and birds galore.

Humans have also made there mark here with graceful dhows with billowing sails, colonial buildings, pulsating nightlife, and a fascinating mix of cultures. Here there are plenty of adventures to be had and attractions to discover.

South Africa

Forget your conceptions about the African continent, South Africa will nullify them. It is a spectacular, exhilarating and complex multicultural nation that is working on overcoming past atrocities.

Called the Rainbow Nation, this name describes not only the people but also the landscapes. You can hike to craggy, snowcapped peaks, sleep under the light of the stars in a russet hued desert or soak up the golden sun on glorious white-sand beaches. Wildlife is also available in many varieties from a eye-opening safari to encounters with waddling penguins.

Tanzania

Home of safaris, with its wide golden plains stampled by wildebeest, elephants kicking up dust, stalking lion prides and hippos jostling in rivers. Yet the country also has sparkling blue lakes, towering peaks, tranquil islands and chimpanzees swinging through lush rainforests.

Tanzania is also steeped in generations of Swahili culture from sleepy villages to bustling cities. You will be greeted by the generosity and warm spirit of the Tanzanians and amazed by their traditions.

Uganda

Dubbed the pearl of Africa, this country is undoubtedly beautiful. Home to The tallest mountain range and the largest lake in Africa as well as the world's longest river along with some superb scenery.

It also boasts it's fair share of challenges to be tackled with chimp tracking in dense forests,mountain gorilla spotting, white water rafting on some wild water and then relaxing on inland beaches.

Zambia

Intriguing due to the natural simplicity inherent in its remote, untamed wilderness regions and tranquil scenery, Zambia offers you many adventures. Hidden in it's wilderness are secret waterfalls, languid rivers and unending bush where animal wander.

Here you can see the amazing Victoria Falls canoe down peaceful rivers and wander where no footprint is to be seen. Ancient cultural ceremonies have been preserved by its people contrasting vividly with its modern cities.
